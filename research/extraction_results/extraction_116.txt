Nigerian Movie Industry Will Fail Without Government Support

The Chief Executive Officer of AK Media Concepts Limited, Mr. Benson Akindeju, producer of television serials, Face-2-Face, Family Ties and No Where To Be Found, has noted that the Nigeria movie industry will fail if it does not get the necessary government support.

He reiterated that although the practitioners have performed excellently well over the years, government's support, which is the only thing lacking, cannot be ignored.

The Nigeria movie industry will fail if government did not support it. In other countries like America, Indian and even South Africa, government give them money to shoot films. They also build film villages for them.

"But here in Nigeria, the reverse is the case as the government is not concerned about the tough challenges being faced by filmmakers," he stated.

Speaking further on the standard of foreign soaps. which are being shown in Nigeria, Mr. Benson said the only edge they have over their Nigerian counterparts is the equipment used in shooting them.

"Foreign soaps are not better. It's just that they have better equipment. Some of them had been shot for years and the picture quality is still okay. This is because government is backing them with funds and other things.

"But if we have half of the support they have, we will be far away, better than them. Because in terms of story line and other things, I think Nigerians are leading the pack," Mr. Benson stressed.

NollywoodReel gathered that AK Media Concept's latest soap entitled No Where To Be Found, will hit the screens in July.

It featured stars like Tony Umez and Eniola Olaniyan among others.

Core Media Storms Nollywood

At a time when movie buffs and critics are lamenting about the poor standard of editing some Nollywood flicks, a new entertainment outfit, Core Media, has stormed the industry with its classy visual and audio editing equipment. Located at Adekunbi Street, Ikeja, Core Media, also has sound and lightening equipment that can handle any kind of event.

Nollywood Reel gathered that the outfit, which handles any media related package for clients, also provides models and ushers for events.

# Summary of Nollywood-Related Information

**Document:** PM News, April 1, 2009

## Key Topics and Details:

### Government Support for Nigerian Film Industry
- **Benson Akindeju**, CEO of AK Media Concepts and producer of *Face-2-Face*, *Family Ties*, and *No Where To Be Found*, stated that the Nigerian movie industry will fail without government support
- He noted that foreign countries (America, India, South Africa) provide government funding and build film villages for filmmakers
- He argued that Nigerian films are comparable in quality to foreign productions in terms of storyline, but lack equipment funding
- AK Media Concepts' latest soap *No Where To Be Found* was scheduled to hit screens in July, featuring Tony Umez and Eniola Olaniyan

### Globacom Ambassadorial Contracts
- Several Nollywood artistes secured ambassadorial contracts with telecommunications company Globacom
- **Mercy Johnson** was initially included but removed from the list following a scandal published by a soft-sell magazine
- **Jim Iyke** was included despite being involved in controversies

### Industry Developments
- **Core Media**, a new entertainment outfit with professional editing equipment, entered the Nollywood industry, located at Adekunbi Street, Ikeja
- **Lagos State Film and Video Censors Board** issued a directive requiring filmmakers using any part of the state to obtain location licenses, expected to reduce poor-quality films

### Other Productions
- **Baba Fash** (Yoruba movie) starring Mercy Aigbe, produced by Afeez Olatunbosun, scheduled for release
- **Omoge Joonu** launching her movie *Aomiloju* at Planet One, Maryland

### Industry Leadership
- **ANTP (Association of Nigerian Theatre Practitioners)** elections underway; **Yemi Solade** contesting for National President position
