**Port Cargo Traffic Increases in Lagos:**

The Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) Managing Director Minudabo conducted a working visit to ports in the Lagos area. N.C. Akani, general manager of Western operations, reported significant increases in traffic at Lagos ports:

**Vessel Traffic:**
- Number of vessels berthing at Lagos ports increased by **48 percent from 1,483 in 1997 to 2,190 in 2001**

**Cargo Traffic:**
- Cargo throughput increased by **116 percent from 8.8 million metric tonnes in 1997 to 19.0 million metric tonnes in 2001**

**Lagos Port Facilities:**
The zone comprises four ports:
1. Lagos Port Complex
2. Container Terminal
3. Tincan Island Port
4. Roll-on Roll-off (RORO) Ports

**Vehicle Traffic:**
Williams Ubong, port manager at RORO Port, stated that the port "presently handles more than 80 percent of vehicles imported into the country through the seaports." He noted that "with the increasing traffic of imported vehicles, the plant situation in RORO Port was grossly inadequate." He also mentioned that "the recent ban on importation of vehicles through the borders of neighbouring countries is likely to further stimulate traffic in the port."

Dabo noted that "the increase in cargo traffics and congestion at the ports were indications of improvement in economic activities."
