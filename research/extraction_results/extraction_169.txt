# Summary of Corruption-Related Content

This is an interview with Pastor Enoch Adeboye, General Overseer of the Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG), published in PM News on September 2, 2008, discussing Nigeria's corruption problem and the anti-graft war.

## Key Points on Corruption:

**Nigeria's Reputation:**
- Adeboye acknowledges Nigeria's reputation as "a nation that is very corrupt"
- He describes the pain of being restricted at foreign airports due to carrying a Nigerian passport
- Recounts an incident where immigration officials demanded he preach a sermon to prove he was a genuine pastor, citing cases of fake Nigerian pastors caught smuggling drugs in hollowed-out Bibles
- States: "In a situation like that you find the innocent suffering because of the misdeeds of a handful of people"

**Assessment of Anti-Corruption Efforts:**
- Praises EFCC's achievements over the previous four years
- Expresses concern about "forces trying to muscle in and slow down the great work"
- Advocates for "an EFCC that is completely autonomous, an EFCC that cannot be manipulated"
- Wants EFCC at the same level as the judiciary, which "can't control judges easily"
- Thanks God for what EFCC and ICPC have accomplished

**Political Corruption:**
- Notes that "about six former state governors" are being tried for corruption and money laundering at the time of the interview
- Acknowledges some of these governors "regularly attended your programmes"

**Church's Role in Fighting Corruption:**
- Explains the church's limited authority: "I can't arrest him; I don't have the power of the police"
- States the church can only preach and encourage people to do what is right
- Describes RCCG's approach: if a pastor is found doing things contrary to his calling, "we don't hide it. If we think he should be handed over to the police, we do that"
- Uses spiritual warnings: tells congregation that money acquired through corruption "can become a curse" and "might turn out to be a weapon in the hand of God to deal with them"

**Church Discipline:**
- RCCG has a discipline system for members found guilty of wrongdoing (not just those being tried)
- Suspension means members "can't take Holy Communion, but he must come to church"
- Suspended members sit in a particular place where "people will know that such person is under suspension"

**Rejecting Corrupt Money:**
- Adeboye shares an example from when RCCG was smaller at Ebute Metta
- A wealthy man donated a public address system, but God told Adeboye: "tell him to take his gift away, I don't want it"
- States: "it is not the first time that somebody will come in and say I want to do things for the church and God will say no don't touch it"
- Emphasizes: "A church of the Redeemed Christian Church of God standing therefore cannot afford to annoy God"

**Root Causes of Corruption:**
- Traces problems to when "teachers were debased"
- Describes how lecturers at University of Lagos were given "48 hours to pack out of our university accommodation"
- This led to lecturers becoming contractors to survive: "everybody is looking for survival"
- Notes that missions used to run schools, but "they threw God out of schools when they took the schools away from the missionaries"
- Economic pressures on families: "in the olden days many a time the father will earn enough to feed the entire family, now papa will work, mama must work"

**Future Prophecy:**
- Predicts: "a day is coming that the same people who are ill treating us abroad when they see the Nigerian passport, will want to touch you because they would have been convinced beyond doubt that God is in Nigeria"

**Final Assessment:**
- Commends EFCC for "taking them [religious people] along"
- Promises: "the almighty God will not allow the good work you have started be destroyed by people who are afraid that if we leave this people alone they might come cracking down on us"

*Note: The interview was culled from "Zero Tolerance," a publication of EFCC.
